## Introduction

### About Your Role

As a **Safety Officer** in the TEMS system, you are responsible for:

- **Incident Management:** Recording, investigating, and resolving safety incidents
- **Safety Inspections:** Conducting and reviewing vehicle and facility safety inspections
- **Compliance Monitoring:** Ensuring adherence to safety regulations and standards
- **Risk Assessment:** Identifying and mitigating safety risks
- **Training:** Managing driver and staff safety training programs
- **Reporting:** Generating safety metrics and compliance reports
- **Investigation:** Analyzing incidents and recommending preventive measures

## Incident Management

### Types of Incidents

**1. Accidents**
- Vehicle collisions
- Injuries to personnel
- Property damage
- Third-party involvement

**2. Near Misses**
- Situations that could have resulted in injury/damage
- No actual harm occurred
- Important for preventive measures

**3. Hazardous Conditions**
- Unsafe vehicle conditions
- Unsafe working conditions
- Environmental hazards

**4. Violations**
- Traffic violations
- Safety procedure violations
- Regulatory non-compliance

### Reporting a New Incident

**Step-by-Step:**

**1. Start New Incident Report**
- Click "üö® Report Incident" on dashboard
- OR Navigate to "Incidents" ‚Üí "New Incident"

**2. Incident Classification**
- **Type:** Select from dropdown
- **Severity:** Critical / High / Medium / Low
- **Date & Time:** When incident occurred
- **Location:** Where incident occurred
- **Status:** Open (auto-set)

**3. Parties Involved**
- **Driver:** Select from dropdown
- **Vehicle:** Select from dropdown
- **Other Parties:** Add if applicable (pedestrians, other vehicles)
- **Witnesses:** Names and contact info

**4. Incident Description**
- **What Happened:** Detailed description
- **Contributing Factors:** Weather, road condition, mechanical, human error
- **Injuries:** Any injuries sustained (describe)
- **Damage:** Vehicle/property damage (describe)
- **Environmental Conditions:** Weather, visibility, road conditions

**5. Documentation**
- **Photos:** Upload photos of scene, damage
- **Police Report:** Attach if applicable
- **Witness Statements:** Attach written statements
- **Medical Reports:** If injuries occurred

**6. Immediate Actions Taken**
- First aid provided?
- Police notified?
- Vehicle immobilized?
- Scene secured?
- Insurance notified?

**7. Submit Report**
- Review all information
- Click "Submit Incident Report"
- System assigns incident number
- Notifications sent to relevant parties

### Investigating an Incident

**Access Investigation:**
1. Navigate to "Incidents"
2. Click incident to open
3. Click "Start Investigation" tab

**Investigation Steps:**

**1. Initial Assessment**
- Review incident report
- Review photos and documentation
- Identify information gaps

**2. Gather Evidence**
- Interview involved parties
- Interview witnesses
- Obtain additional documentation
- Review vehicle maintenance records
- Review driver history
- Check route conditions

**3. Root Cause Analysis**
Use 5 Whys method:
- Why did incident occur?
- Why did that happen?
- Continue until root cause identified

**4. Contributing Factors**
Identify all factors:
- Human factors (fatigue, distraction, error)
- Vehicle factors (mechanical failure, maintenance)
- Environmental factors (weather, road conditions)
- Organizational factors (policies, training, procedures)

**5. Findings & Conclusions**
- Summary of investigation
- Root cause determination
- Contributing factors identified
- Responsibility assignment (if applicable)

**6. Recommendations**
- Corrective actions
- Preventive measures
- Policy changes needed
- Training requirements
- Disciplinary actions (if warranted)

**7. Close Investigation**
- Complete all sections
- Attach final report
- Set implementation timeline for recommendations
- Assign action items to responsible parties
- Click "Close Investigation"

### Tracking Incident Resolution

**Monitor Progress:**
1. Navigate to "Incidents" ‚Üí "Open Incidents"
2. Click incident
3. View "Action Items" tab
4. Track completion status
5. Add follow-up notes
6. Close incident when all actions complete

## Safety Inspections

### Types of Inspections

**1. Pre-Trip Inspections**
- Conducted before every journey
- Basic safety checks
- Pass/fail criteria
- Linked to journey plans

**2. Periodic Inspections**
- Monthly/quarterly scheduled inspections
- Comprehensive safety audit
- Regulatory compliance focus

**3. Random Inspections**
- Unscheduled spot checks
- Quality assurance
- Compliance verification

**4. Post-Incident Inspections**
- After accident or breakdown
- Determine vehicle safety status
- Required before return to service

### Conducting a Safety Inspection

**Starting Inspection:**
1. Navigate to "Inspections" ‚Üí "New Inspection"
2. Select inspection type
3. Select vehicle
4. System loads appropriate checklist

**Safety Inspection Checklist:**

**A. Structural Safety (8 items)**
- Frame integrity
- Body panels secure
- No sharp edges/protrusions
- Floor integrity
- Emergency exits operational
- Windows intact, not cracked

**B. Fire Safety (6 items)**
- Fire extinguisher present
- Fire extinguisher valid (not expired)
- Fire extinguisher accessible
- No flammable materials stored unsafely
- Electrical wiring safe (no exposed wires)
- Fuel system secure (no leaks)

**C. Emergency Equipment (10 items)**
- First aid kit complete
- First aid kit not expired
- Warning triangle (2 required)
- Reflective vest for driver
- Emergency tool kit
- Spare tire in good condition
- Jack and lug wrench
- Emergency contact list posted
- Fire evacuation plan posted (for buses)
- Emergency hammer (for buses)

**D. Passenger Safety (Buses) (8 items)**
- Seatbelts present and functional (all seats)
- Handrails secure
- Steps/entry secure
- Emergency door operational
- Interior lighting working
- No loose objects
- Aisle clear
- Capacity sign posted

**E. Driver Safety (6 items)**
- Driver seat secure and adjustable
- Seatbelt functional
- Mirrors properly adjusted
- Clear visibility (no obstructions)
- Controls accessible
- Instruments readable

**F. Regulatory Compliance (5 items)**
- Vehicle registration current
- Insurance certificate current
- Roadworthiness certificate current
- Permit documents present
- Inspection stickers current

**For Each Item:**
- ‚úÖ Pass - Meets safety standards
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Advisory - Attention needed soon (not critical)
- ‚ùå Fail - Does not meet standards (critical)
- N/A - Not applicable to this vehicle

**Adding Notes:**
- Describe any deficiencies
- Attach photos
- Note corrective action required

**Completing Inspection:**
1. Complete all applicable items
2. Calculate overall score
3. Determine result:
   - **Pass:** No critical deficiencies
   - **Conditional Pass:** Advisory items only, corrective action within 30 days
   - **Fail:** One or more critical deficiencies, vehicle grounded
4. Add inspector notes
5. Digital signature
6. Submit inspection

**Post-Submission:**
- Inspection linked to vehicle record
- If fail: Vehicle marked "Out of Service"
- Fleet team notified of deficiencies
- Follow-up inspection scheduled
- Compliance score updated

## Risk Assessment

### Risk Categories

**1. Operational Risks**
- Route hazards
- Weather conditions
- Traffic conditions
- Time pressures

**2. Vehicle Risks**
- Mechanical condition
- Age of vehicle
- Maintenance history
- Previous incidents

**3. Personnel Risks**
- Driver experience
- Fatigue
- Medical conditions
- Training gaps

**4. External Risks**
- Road conditions
- Crime/security
- Natural disasters
- Third-party actions

### Conducting Risk Assessment

**Starting Assessment:**
1. Navigate to "Risk Assessment" ‚Üí "New Assessment"
2. Select context:
   - Route-specific
   - Vehicle-specific
   - Driver-specific
   - General operational

**Assessment Process:**

**1. Identify Hazards**
List all potential hazards:
- What could go wrong?
- What are the dangers?
- What situations could cause harm?

**2. Assess Likelihood**
For each hazard, rate likelihood:
- **Rare:** Unlikely to occur
- **Possible:** Could occur sometimes
- **Likely:** Will probably occur
- **Almost Certain:** Expected to occur often

**3. Assess Severity**
For each hazard, rate potential impact:
- **Minor:** Small impact, easily recovered
- **Moderate:** Noticeable impact, manageable
- **Major:** Significant impact, serious consequences
- **Catastrophic:** Severe impact, potential loss of life

**4. Calculate Risk Level**
System calculates:
```
Risk = Likelihood √ó Severity

Risk Levels:
- Low (1-4): Accept with monitoring
- Medium (5-9): Implement controls
- High (10-16): Immediate action required
- Critical (17-25): Stop activity until mitigated
```

**5. Control Measures**
For each risk, identify controls:
- **Eliminate:** Remove the hazard
- **Substitute:** Replace with safer alternative
- **Engineering Controls:** Physical barriers, guards
- **Administrative Controls:** Procedures, training
- **PPE:** Personal protective equipment

**6. Residual Risk**
After controls, re-assess:
- What is remaining risk level?
- Is it acceptable?
- What monitoring is needed?

**7. Action Plan**
- List all control measures
- Assign responsibilities
- Set timelines
- Allocate resources

**8. Submit Assessment**
- Review all sections
- Attach supporting documentation
- Submit for approval
- System adds to risk register

### Managing Risk Register

**View Risk Register:**
1. Navigate to "Risk Assessment" ‚Üí "Risk Register"

   - Risk level
   - Category
   - Status
   - Date identified

**Review Risks:**
- Quarterly review minimum
- Review after incidents
- Update control measures
- Close mitigated risks

## FAQ

**Q: What is the difference between incident and near miss?**  
A: Incident resulted in actual harm/damage. Near miss had potential for harm but no actual harm occurred. Both must be reported.

**Q: How often should safety inspections be conducted?**  
A: Pre-trip inspections before every journey. Periodic inspections monthly or as per schedule. Random inspections as needed.

**Q: Can I override a failed inspection?**  
A: No. Failed inspections cannot be overridden. Vehicle must be repaired and re-inspected.

**Q: Who is notified when I report an incident?**  
A: Operations Manager, Fleet Manager (if vehicle involved), driver's supervisor, and system administrator.

**Q: How long do I have to investigate an incident?**  
A: Critical incidents: 24 hours. High severity: 48 hours. Medium/Low: 7 days. Can request extension if needed.

**Q: What happens if compliance score drops below 95%?**  
A: System flags for management review. You'll receive notification to address non-compliant items. May trigger audit.
